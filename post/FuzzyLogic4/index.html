Fuzzy Logic with Engineering Applications, Chapter 4

Summary

  • Membership function
    • Core The region with complete membership \mu_A(x)=1
    • Support The region with nonzero membership (so basically the entire membership?) \mu_A(x)>0
    • Boundaries More than zero less than one. basically some degree of membership other than 1 0<\mu_A(x)<1
  • Fuzzy Sets
    • Normal At least one member has a membership value of 1
      • prototype In a normal set with exactly one element with a membership value of one, that element is called the prototype
    • Convex Goes up, then down once, no crazy zigzags. Seems to be the sames thing as “normal”
    • generalized membership function different from the more or less normal looking sloped forms. Looks like a double parabola.
      * interval-valued membership function A subset of generalized membership function. * type 2 fuzzy set If you allow the intervals to become fuzzy then the membership function is referred to as a type 2
  • Fuzzification Making “crisp” data fuzzy. Most common method seems to be to pick your zero and unity values, and use linear interpolation.
  • Defuzzification Putting back after you do stuff to it. There are a bunch of methods for this
    • Lambda Cut Precursor to all other defuzzification operations. Take a fuzzy set and pick a lambda value from 0 to 1 representing grade of membership. Any member that’s higher passes, any lower don’t. Do this a bunch of times at different values and you get an idea how the members are distributed. Reminds me a lot of A to D conversion. You can do it for relations too. I’m going to pretend I understand that part.
    • 1. Max membership principle
    • 2. Centroid Method
    • 3. Weighted Average Method
    • 4. Mean Max Membership
    • 5. Center of sums
    • 6. Center of largest area
    • 7. First (or last) of Maxima
